The Question Nobody Was Asking

Every platform solved the wrong problem.

Every travel platform is built around the same assumption: the hard part is finding the right destination. Get that right and the rest follows. So platforms optimise for discovery: better search, better filters, better inspiration feeds.

The research said something different. Across 170 respondents and 5 in-depth interviews, the finding was consistent enough to be uncomfortable: the hardest part of group travel is not finding a destination. It is deciding who to travel with, and more specifically, committing to those people before you know enough to be sure.

Research

Two surveys, five interviews, eight platforms.

Survey A reached 140 respondents and focused on travel preferences, pace, and compatibility signals. Survey B reached 30 respondents and went deeper on friction points, safety behaviour, and emotional drivers. Five in-depth interviews with solo and semi-solo travellers covered journey mapping from discovery through post-trip reflection. Competitive analysis spanned eight platforms including GAFFL, Tourlina, Backpackr, and TripGiraffe.

The research focus was deliberately narrow: not how people book, not where people want to go - specifically, how do people decide who to travel with, and where does that decision break down?

Three Findings

What the research would not let us ignore.

Finding 01
Commitment, not ideation
70% hesitate when money, time, and people get locked in together. Designing for faster discovery solves the wrong problem.
Finding 02
Same destination is a weak proxy
Platforms match on where and when. Users fail on how - pace, energy, budget, activities. Entirely predictable upfront if you capture the right signals.
Finding 03
Safety is a baseline
3 in 4 women did manual trust vetting outside platforms. Users were already doing the work. No platform supported it.
Core Insight

Social friction, not technical friction.

"Tools for booking exist. Tools for aligning humans before commitment do not."

Planning friction in group travel is social, not technical. The opportunity is not a better discovery feed. It is a pre-commitment decision system that helps users accumulate enough signal about the people they might travel with to say yes confidently, or no early enough that it does not cost them anything.

Who We Designed For

Riya needs confidence. Priya needs safety. Both need the platform to do the trust work.

Riya is 26, a Product Manager in Bengaluru. She wants weekend trips to Coorg and Goa. Her friends have moved away. The problem is not finding people. The problem is knowing whether those people are the right ones before it is too late to back out. Her words: "I do not need more matches. I need to know if we will actually get along on the trip."

Priya is 35, a teacher in Mumbai. She needs clear intent before she engages. She prefers public meetings before any commitment. One uncomfortable experience is enough to leave a platform entirely. Both personas represent the same underlying need at different intensity levels. Both need the platform to do the trust work that is currently happening manually outside any app.

The System

8 steps. One recurring question: do I have enough confidence to move forward?

1
Onboarding
Travel style, budget bands, pace, social energy. Non-negotiables captured privately as silent hard filters. Riya never has to be the difficult one.
2
Compatibility Engine
Runs across travel style, pace, budget, activity preferences, lifestyle signals. Compatibility is how people travel, not where they want to go.
3
Intent Declaration
Users declare a specific trip with destination, dates, and rough budget. Gates access to matching. Filters casual browsers before any matching resources are spent.
4
Matching with Reasons
Maximum five matches per intent. Every match comes with an explicit explanation. Quality replaces volume.
5
Structured Chat
Unlocks only after mutual acceptance. Responses are time-bound with a 72-hour expiry. Filters users who match but never follow through.
6
Public-First Meeting
Enforced structurally. Private addresses are blocked. The platform prompts public venue suggestions. Safety enforced structurally, not socially.
7
Active Trip Safety
One-tap SOS, pre-loaded emergency contacts, optional live location sharing, automated check-ins.
8
Feedback Loop
Post-trip ratings feed the algorithm, build trust scores, improve match quality over time.
Three Design Decisions

The calls worth explaining.

Verification improves visibility but does not gate participation. Verified users appear first in matches and get a trust badge, but unverified users can still participate. Safety through transparency, not gatekeeping.

Maximum five matches per intent. Every competing platform optimises for volume. The research showed the opposite: too many matches without enough signal creates the same commitment anxiety the platform is trying to solve.

Non-negotiables are silent. Captured privately and run as hard filters. Users get the filtering benefit without the social cost of disclosure.
